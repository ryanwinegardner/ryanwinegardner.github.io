Collected Haiku

Haiku from my first year of college

I began writing haiku during my first year of college. Because I entered in the fall of 2020, this was during the height of the pandemic. What I thought a first year of college was “supposed” to look like were fundamentally uprooted. It was hard to make friends. My classes were all virtual. For a teenager fresh out of high school and fed on dreams of the good life that college represents, this was a major disapointment that I struggled to overcome. I was often bored and lonely and sad because I felt like I had missed two of the most important phases of my life to a pandemic: my senior year of high school and my first year of college.

Sometime in September, I discovered Issa and Basho, and soon bought several of their haiku books. I must've read them each five or six times, my first semester. And they inspired me of a way to deal with what I was feeling. When I had nothing else to do I would walk alone across grounds and try to find beauty, humor, anything to make this college experience feel meaningful at all.

I follow a haiku tradition in which a haiku is three lines short enough to be said in just one breath.

losing his antlers
the buck enters the ark
slowly…

The third snow arrives!
to empty streets
and closed doors

A crane's reflection --
What does he see? His beaks
merge, and produce two fish.

Just after the rain –
a hawk sits on the roof
and prays.

The bus is also
going nowhere
in this warm night.

Wondering when it will end –
the guitar whispers,
the night bus passes.

Little spider’s web
holding the bridge
together

It’s harvest season –
I’m stuffing myself with
microwave mac and cheese

Crazy autumn moon!
Don’t you know
You’ll never catch the sun?

4 flights of concrete stairs,
a backpack
full of beer

Friday morning –
I can’t catch up
to my shadow.

My shadow carries me
down the street...
early morning blues.

Hello mosquito –
What are you
here to say?

It’s late Mr. Mosquito –
tell me your news
and go

They tell me grand things
but I just don’t care…
napping in the Parthenon.

Peace and Love, RTW

Haiku from my third year of college.

Having returned to the University of Virginia after a summer working in Morocco, I had a new perspective on what I wanted out of college. I had made friends who fundamentally shifted the way I wanted to be. This year became a year of self-contemplation, of reflection. I no longer felt the compulsion to color my world with beauty as a way to combat the ugliness of my life. Two years of haiku writing, of appreciating the phenomenal, had brought beauty into my world.

Why does the sunset
only shine on the
highest trees?

The cow meets
othercow, drinking
pond water.

The not-leaves in the tree
quiet when I approach
and take flight.

A thousand blackbirds
twirl like not-snow
on not-wind.

The old hawk
(without moving)
glides across the sky.

The buzzard on the fence
shifts from one foot
to the other.

It’s november –
and the bugs chirp
like alone on a stage.

Without noise,
without confusion –
november rain.

Our footsteps whisper
across vigil grounds
like november rain.

This sky meets
another sky –
so still is the creek.

My reflection –
obscured by a
condom in the water.

Maple branches
like cracks in the
china blue sky.

These maple branches
are cracks
in the china blue sky.

Finding between two branches
a hypotenuse!
the squirrel leaps.

The winter sunset
burns so bright
but warms nothing...

The burning winter sunset
is enough warmth
for me!

Winter trees
and their shadows
like prison bars.

Winter trees
and their shadows
like new alphabets!

A slagmetal sunset
like in a world yet
becoming.

The poet’s shadow
contemplates him
just the same.

Outside the laughter –
a form kneels
in his own vomit...

Under the cherry blossoms –
a mother kneels
before her child.

Image from a distant past –
she picks flowers
in the garden.

Midnight –
the SUV glides
without noise.

Warm spring day –
the gnats swarm
like a thunderhead.

One more cup of coffee
while I listen to
rain on the window...

Overcome by
passing beauty!
An afternoon rainstorm.

The cosmos glimmers
through my old
whiskey bottles.

In the honeysuckle shades
a squirrel
stalks its prey...

In the sedge grass
two squirrels circle
as in a ritual...

an ancient ritual –
two squirrels circle
in daffodil leaves.

Just before the storm
the crow dances
with two jays.

Just before the storm
two jays squawk
at the praying crow.

Katoubia mosque
in the red dusk –
even the dog wails!

In the shadow
of medina walls
a stray cat sleeps...

A weary ant
riding my backpack
to the next stop...

Starlings in the dusk
like magic stars
in magic skies.

Like the dance of the planets
starlings circle
in the dusk.

The Frog King
surveys his domain
from a throne of rock.

Springtime –
A salamander
sleeping on the pond-floor.

While she picks flowers
the frog croaks
and croaks and croaks...

The croaking frog
perches on his rock –
the whole pond stops to listen.

We sit under
magnolia shade...
the flowers bloom so briefly!

Where the hills are gentle
and the grasses sweet –
spending time with you...

Hiding from the rain
these columns, too,
don’t seem to end...

The bullfrog and I
staring at each other
from across the pond.

Where grass ripples like water
and sunshine melts into
Blueridge mountain mist...

Holiness –
laughter chimes
throughout the garden...

Two blue jays in the orchard –
renegade lovers
under apple tree shade.
